NFT Race Car

"The Bot Market Is Crashing"

While doing research on the shoe bots we learned that the bot market was crashing, through the summer Popular Hypebeast brands such as Yeezy Supplies, Adidas, Nike, Supreme and Shopify, where all moving away from the traditional first come first serve. Instead these sites were moving towards waiting rooms and giving the shoes out at random. When the drop comes at 10am everyone floods into the waiting room. From here the shoes are given out at random regardless of how fast you join the queue. This makes botting obsolete, paired with our original doubt we've decided to switch our project idea and go fourth with Nft Car Racing.

Nft Car Racing

When you first come to the site you will notice a few things: the shop, mechanic, racing and your garage. The first thing you'll want to do when visiting the site will be to make an account and buy a car. Cars will be released Once in a while programmed with a top speed, name, and color. You can view your car and make changes to it in your garage. Okay Great, we have our first car, Now we are able to participate in races. We have two options: Create a race, or join a race. To create a race you will choose an entry fee and the race distance, ¼ mile, ½ mile, 1 mile races. To join a race (and create) you need to pay the entry fee chosen by whoever created the race. This can range from $1 to $100. The entry fees are added up into the prize pool which will be split by our winners later. With 10 cars on the starting line the Prize will be split between winners 1, 2 and 3. 1st place will receive 70% of the prize pool, 2nd place will receive 20% and 3rd gets 10% (their money back). One factor we made sure to keep in mind is hacking/cheats, in order to combat this we've made there is no user interaction in the race, nothing the player does can affect the outcome of the race, because of this there is no room for hacks/cheats. Now we know how to buy a car, and how to race, but how can we make our car faster? If we head back to the shop you will be able to find car parts, from wheels to turbos to nitrous, all affecting the car in one way or another. Once you have purchased the parts you can install them by sending your car to the Mechanic for a small fee. While your car is with the mechanic you will not be able to use the car in any races for an amount of time depending on the upgrade.

Full Circle

Since These Cars are Nfts there are a limited number of them. Once all the cars have been sold they can be resold on 3rd party sites such as opensea.io, The prices on here would be set by whoever owns the Nfts. You can purchase the cars from this site with Ethereum.

Finish Line

In order to make sure we get through this project at a consistent rate we have made a bunch of steps in order to stay on track. Our first goal is to start with a simple game such as tic-tac-toe played on two separate computers, just to make sure we have the multiplayer aspect incorporated. In the beginning we will be starting as simple as red car vs blue car, red cars top speed is 240mph and blue cars top speed is 100mph. Since the deciding factor of which cars wins the race is top speed the red car would obviously win. With these small steps we hope to have a working version of the game without any money involved by the end of the school year.
